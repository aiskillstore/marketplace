# Constitutional Frameworks Reference

## Common Constitutional Elements

### 1. Mission & Purpose
- **Definition**: Why the organization exists
- **Key Questions**:
  - What problem do we solve?
  - Who do we serve?
  - What is our fundamental purpose?
- **Examples**:
  - "To organize the world's information"
  - "To create innovative technology solutions"

### 2. Vision & Aspiration
- **Definition**: Where the organization is going
- **Key Questions**:
  - What future do we want to create?
  - How will the world be different?
  - What is our ultimate aspiration?
- **Examples**:
  - "A world where everyone has access to information"
  - "To be the global leader in sustainable technology"

### 3. Core Values
- **Definition**: What principles guide our actions
- **Common Values**:
  - Integrity, Excellence, Innovation
  - Customer Focus, Teamwork, Responsibility
  - Transparency, Accountability, Respect

### 4. Guiding Principles
- **Definition**: How we operate and make decisions
- **Categories**:
  - Operational principles
  - Ethical principles
  - Strategic principles

### 5. Governance Structure
- **Definition**: How authority is distributed
- **Elements**:
  - Decision-making processes
  - Leadership structure
  - Accountability mechanisms
  - Communication channels

### 6. Quality Standards
- **Definition**: How we measure success
- **Types**:
  - Performance metrics
  - Quality criteria
  - Success benchmarks
  - Excellence definitions

## Constitutional Patterns in Documents

### Mission Statements Often Contain:
- "We exist to..."
- "Our purpose is..."
- "We are here to..."
- "The reason we exist..."
- "We aim to..."

### Vision Statements Often Contain:
- "We envision..."
- "Our vision is..."
- "We will create..."
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "The future of..."

### Values Often Expressed As:
- "We believe in..."
- "We value..."
- "We are committed to..."
- "We stand for..."
- "Our principles include..."

### Governance Indicators:
- "Decisions are made by..."
- "We operate through..."
- "Authority rests with..."
- "We are accountable to..."
- "Leadership means..."

### Quality Indicators:
- "Success is measured by..."
- "We define excellence as..."
- "Quality means..."
- "We track..."
- "Performance is evaluated based on..."

## Document Types & Constitutional Content

### Charters
- Often contain mission, vision, and governance
- Look for sections titled "Purpose," "Scope," "Authority"

### Strategic Plans
- Rich in vision and strategic principles
- Check "Goals," "Objectives," "Strategic Direction"

### Policy Documents
- Source of governance and decision processes
- Look for "Procedures," "Guidelines," "Policies"

### Company Handbooks
- Usually contain values and cultural principles
- Check "Culture," "Values," "Code of Conduct"

### Project Plans
- May contain mission and quality standards
- Look for "Project Purpose," "Success Criteria"

## Extraction Tips

1. **Look for explicit headings**: Documents often label constitutional sections
2. **Identify stakeholder references**: "For our customers," "For our team"
3. **Find action statements**: "We will," "We are," "We believe"
4. **Note measurement language**: "Success is," "We measure," "KPIs include"
5. **Spot commitment phrases**: "We are committed to," "We promise"
