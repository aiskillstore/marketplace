# Glossary

Use this reference when a reader, reviewer, engineer, journalist, or grant evaluator needs the stack terms in plain language.

## Afrotemporalism

The temporal frame in Cook-Kendrick's dissertation: time is spiral, not linear. Present care, ancestral memory, cultural context, and future self-recognition are held together.

## Diasporic Intelligence

Afrotemporalism in Action: the structured, consent-governed transmission of cultural, spiritual, and lineage wisdom across generations through registered voice, dialogic AI, and community accountability.

Plain-language version: a consent-based framework for using culturally rooted human knowledge in AI systems with traceable source attribution, provenance, and revocation.

## DIG Method

Digital Identity Gathering. The consent-based protocol for documenting a registrant's surface, subterranean, and root memory layers and rendering them into a Voice Bible.

## Voice Bible

The registrant's personality, cadence, boundaries, response logic, and relational pattern specification. It constrains how a Digital Self or Digital Double may respond.

## ZORAs

The personalization-with-provenance layer named in honor of Zora Neale Hurston. It grounds responses in the registrant's own archive and consent state.

## Digital Self / Digital Double

A consent-governed dialogic likeness that can reflect a registrant's authored voice, memory, boundaries, and wisdom. It is a scaffold or mirror, not a person, ancestor, clinician, pastor, or deity.

## Sisterhood Voice Data Trust

The governance and accountability structure for registered voice and related voice-governance artifacts in the Much Different World ecosystem.

## Much Different World

The voice registry and protection platform built by Sisterhood Sit-In, LLC.

## Registered voice

Voice treated as a consent-governed identity layer. It may include audio, text, transcript, cadence, boundary, memory, register, and source identity. Biometric audio capture is only one possible modality and requires explicit consent.

## Dialogic AI

AI used as through-speech with a human source under disclosure, consent, provenance, and revocation. In implementation terms, it is a conversational system with consent-gated access to an authored or authorized corpus, provenance tracking, and revocation-aware data controls. It is different from generic generative AI because the human source remains the author of meaning.

## Governance primitives

The minimum operational parts that keep Diasporic Intelligence from becoming extraction: source attribution, consent state, provenance metadata, revocation or unbinding, role boundaries, auditability, and community accountability.

## Black Quantum Futurism

An interdisciplinary creative practice founded by Rasheedah Phillips and Camae Ayewa. It is an adjacent root for Black temporal theory and practice, not a synonym for Diasporic Intelligence.
