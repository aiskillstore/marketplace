## Voice Characteristics in Action

### Professional but Approachable

**Good:** "We're here to help you succeed. Our expert team has years of experience, and we're ready to share that knowledge with you."

**Too Formal:** "It is the intention of this organization to facilitate your success through the provision of professional services by our experienced personnel."

**Too Casual:** "Hey! We're total pros and we wanna help you crush it! Hit us up anytime!"

### Clear and Direct

**Good:** "Save 30% on your subscription when you upgrade today."

**Too Vague:** "There may be opportunities for cost optimization through strategic purchasing decisions."

**Too Blunt:** "Stop wasting money. Upgrade now."

### Authentic and Confident

**Good:** "We've helped thousands of businesses streamline their operations. We're confident we can help you too."

**Not Confident:** "We think we might be able to help you, maybe."

**Arrogant:** "We're the best solution on the market. Everyone else is just copying us."

## Technical Documentation

### ‚ùå BEFORE (Off-Brand)
```

in the upper-right quadrant of the interface. This will allow
the system settings to be accessed.

Just navigate to the preferences section and make your changes.

```

**Problems:**
- Passive voice
- Words like "simply" and "just" (assume ease)
- Imprecise language ("upper-right quadrant")
- Patronizing tone ("It's easy!")

### ‚úì AFTER (On-Brand)
```
To access settings:

1. Click the gear icon in the top-right corner
2. Select "Preferences" from the dropdown menu
3. Make your changes and click "Save"

Tip: You can also use the keyboard shortcut Cmd+, (Mac)
or Ctrl+, (Windows) to open settings quickly.
```

**Improvements:**
- Clear numbered steps
- Precise language
- Active voice
- Helpful additional information
- No assumptions about difficulty

## Key Takeaways

### Our Brand Voice Is:
- **Professional yet approachable:** Expert without being stuffy
- **Clear and direct:** Simple language, concrete benefits
- **Authentic:** Honest, transparent, human
- **Confident:** Knowledgeable without arrogance
- **Customer-focused:** "You" not "we"

### We Avoid:
- Corporate jargon and buzzwords
- Excessive hype and superlatives
- Passive voice
- Assumptions ("simply," "just")
- Fear-based urgency
- Impersonal, robotic language

### We Use:
- Active voice
- Second person ("you")
- Specific, concrete examples
- Contractions (when appropriate)
- Clear, scannable formatting
- Genuine warmth and empathy
