## Template Usage Notes

### When to Use This Template

**Frequency Options**:
- **End of Sprint** (most common): Comprehensive sprint summary
- **Weekly**: Lighter version for fast-moving teams
- **Bi-weekly**: Balanced approach for 2-week sprints
- **Ad-hoc**: For major milestones or significant updates

### Customization by Team Type

**Engineering Teams**:
- Emphasize technical decisions and architecture
- Include code quality metrics
- Highlight infrastructure improvements
- Detail technical debt management

**Product Teams**:
- Focus on user impact and feedback
- Emphasize feature adoption metrics
- Include product analytics
- Highlight customer conversations

**Design Teams**:
- Showcase research findings
- Include design iterations and feedback
- Highlight user testing results
- Show design system contributions

**Operations Teams**:
- Focus on process efficiency
- Highlight incident response and prevention
- Show SLA/SLO metrics
- Emphasize automation and optimization

### Length Guidelines

**Full Version** (recommended for bi-weekly/sprint end):
- Use all sections as appropriate
- ~1500-2000 words
- Comprehensive documentation
- Suitable for stakeholder updates

**Quick Version** (for weekly updates):
- Use only: At a Glance, Wins, Challenges, Upcoming Work
- ~500-750 words
- Focuses on progress and priorities
- Great for team-internal updates

**Executive Summary** (for leadership):
- Extract: Status, Major Wins, Critical Blockers, Key Metrics
- ~300 words
- Focus on outcomes and business impact
- Include asks or decisions needed

### Best Practices

**Writing Tips**:
- Be specific: "Improved load time by 40%" not "Made it faster"
- Credit people: Tag team members by name for contributions
- Be honest: Share challenges as well as wins
- Be visual: Use emoji, tables, and formatting for scanability
- Link liberally: Connect to detailed docs, PRs, dashboards

**Distribution**:
- **Team Channel**: Post in team Slack channel
- **Stakeholder Email**: Send to interested parties outside team
- **Wiki Archive**: Maintain historical record
- **Cross-Team Channels**: Share highlights in relevant channels
- **All-Hands**: Pull into company-wide updates when appropriate

**Timing**:
- Post within 24 hours of sprint end/week end
- Consistent schedule (every Friday, last day of sprint, etc.)
- Morning posting (higher visibility) vs. end of day

**Engagement**:
- Encourage team to read and comment
- Ask for input on open questions
- Celebrate wins together
- Use reactions/emoji for quick engagement

### Automation Opportunities

Consider automating data for:
- Sprint metrics (from Jira/Linear/etc.)
- Deployment frequency (from CI/CD)
- Test coverage (from testing tools)
- Bug counts (from issue tracker)
- Velocity charts (from project management tool)

### Rotating Ownership

Benefits of rotating who writes the update:
- Develops communication skills across team
- Provides different perspectives
- Reduces bottleneck on single person
- Increases engagement with update format

**Rotation Schedule**:
- Alphabetical by name
- Rotate each sprint/week
- Provide template and examples
- Allow 30 minutes in calendar for writing
