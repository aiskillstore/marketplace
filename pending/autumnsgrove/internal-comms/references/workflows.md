# Detailed Communication Workflows

This document provides step-by-step workflows for creating different types of internal communications.

## Creating Regular Status Reports

### Step 1: Preparation (5 minutes)
- Review previous status report
- Check metrics dashboard
- Review completed work (tickets, PRs, documents)
- Note any blockers or risks
- Identify upcoming priorities

### Step 2: Draft Content (15 minutes)
- Start with executive summary (write this last)
- Fill in metrics with context
- List accomplishments with impact
- Document challenges honestly
- Outline next priorities
- Add action items with owners

### Step 3: Add Context (10 minutes)
- Explain significant metric changes
- Provide links to relevant documents
- Credit team members by name
- Connect work to company goals
- Add helpful screenshots or visuals

### Step 4: Review and Refine (5 minutes)
- Check for clarity and conciseness
- Ensure action items are specific
- Verify all links work
- Proofread for errors
- Confirm consistent formatting

### Step 5: Distribute (2 minutes)
- Send at consistent time
- Post in relevant Slack channels
- Tag relevant stakeholders
- Archive in shared location
- Add to status report index

**Time Commitment**:
- Weekly: ~30-40 minutes
- Becomes faster with consistent format
- Can reduce to 20 minutes once routine

## Crafting Engaging Newsletters

### Step 1: Content Collection (Ongoing)
- Set up submission form for content
- Monitor Slack channels for wins
- Track launches and milestones
- Collect photos and media
- Note upcoming events
- Solicit team spotlights monthly

### Step 2: Content Selection (20 minutes)
- Review all submitted content
- Choose diverse content (different teams/topics)
- Prioritize time-sensitive items
- Balance serious and fun content
- Select strong visuals

### Step 3: Writing and Design (45 minutes)
- Write compelling headlines
- Craft engaging opening
- Write each section with personality
- Add images and graphics
- Ensure mobile-friendly layout
- Include clear calls-to-action

### Step 4: Review Process (30 minutes)
- Get leadership approval (if needed)
- Verify all facts and dates
- Check all links work
- Test on mobile device
- Proofread carefully
- Get second set of eyes

### Step 5: Distribution and Follow-up (15 minutes)
- Send at optimal time (Tuesday-Thursday morning)
- Post excerpts in Slack
- Pin for visibility
- Track open and click rates
- Solicit feedback
- Archive for future reference

**Time Commitment**:
- Weekly newsletter: ~2 hours
- Bi-weekly newsletter: ~3 hours (more content)
- Monthly newsletter: ~4 hours (more comprehensive)

**Optimization Tips**:
- Use templates to speed up design
- Batch write multiple sections
- Delegate section ownership
- Reuse format and structure
- Build content library over time

## Writing Clear Announcements

### Step 1: Preparation
- Clarify the decision/change to announce
- Understand the rationale deeply
- Identify all affected groups
- Anticipate reactions and concerns
- Gather supporting data/documentation
- Align with leadership team

### Step 2: Structure the Message
- Write TL;DR first (the headline)
- Outline key sections
- Plan FAQ section
- Determine appropriate length
- Choose tone and formality level

### Step 3: Draft Content
- Start with clear, direct summary
- Provide necessary context
- Explain the what, why, and when
- Address "what this means for you"
- Include specific next steps
- Add FAQ section
- Provide contact for questions

### Step 4: Anticipate Reactions
- Consider different perspectives
- Address concerns proactively
- Prepare managers with talking points
- Plan for follow-up discussions
- Have escalation plan for issues

### Step 5: Review and Approve
- Get leadership sign-off
- Legal review (if needed)
- HR review (if people-related)
- Test on sample audience
- Finalize timing

### Step 6: Multi-Channel Distribution
- Email to all-company
- Post in Slack #announcements
- Discuss in all-hands meeting
- Publish on company wiki
- Schedule manager talking points
- Plan follow-up communications

### Step 7: Monitor and Respond
- Watch for questions and concerns
- Respond quickly and thoughtfully
- Collect feedback
- Adjust messaging if needed
- Schedule follow-up update

**Critical Timing Considerations**:
- Avoid Friday afternoons (gets lost over weekend)
- Avoid Monday mornings (competes with week planning)
- Best: Tuesday-Thursday 10am-2pm
- Allow time for questions before end of day
- Consider global time zones

## Structuring Team Updates

### Weekly Team Update Workflow

**Step 1: Data Collection (Automated)**
- Pull sprint/project metrics
- Review completed tickets/PRs
- Check velocity and burndown
- Note any incidents or issues
- Track upcoming deadlines

**Step 2: Team Input (Async)**
- Ask team for notable wins
- Solicit challenges or blockers
- Request help needed
- Gather learning moments
- Collect shout-outs

**Step 3: Synthesis (15 minutes)**
- Identify key themes
- Highlight most impactful work
- Note patterns in challenges
- Connect work to goals
- Prepare forward-looking items

**Step 4: Write Update (20 minutes)**
- Craft engaging opening
- Summarize sprint progress
- Celebrate wins with specifics
- Address challenges transparently
- Preview upcoming work
- Add team shout-outs

**Step 5: Share and Discuss (5 minutes)**
- Post in team channel
- Share with stakeholders
- Discuss in team meeting
- Invite questions
- Archive for reference

**Rotating Ownership**:
- Rotate who writes update each week
- Provides different perspectives
- Develops communication skills
- Increases team engagement
- Reduces bottleneck on manager

**Template Customization**:
- Engineering: Focus on technical achievements, tech debt, incidents
- Product: Emphasize user impact, feature launches, feedback
- Design: Highlight research, designs, user testing
- Operations: Showcase efficiency gains, process improvements

**Phase 4: Post-Mortem (Within 2-5 days)**

Full post-mortem following blameless format:
- Timeline of events
- Root cause analysis
- Impact assessment
- What went well
- What could be improved
- Action items with owners
- Follow-up date

**Communication Roles**:
- **Incident Commander**: Makes technical decisions
- **Communications Lead**: Writes updates (dedicated role)
- **Customer Communications**: Handles external updates
- **Executive Liaison**: Briefs leadership

**Best Practices**:
- Stick to regular update schedule
- Be honest about uncertainty
- Don't speculate on causes initially
- Keep technical and exec summaries separate
- Archive all communications
- Follow up on all action items

## Feedback Collection Mechanisms

### Methods for Gathering Communication Feedback

**1. Anonymous Surveys (Quarterly)**
```
Communication Effectiveness Survey:

1. Do you feel well-informed about company direction? (1-5)
2. Are status reports useful and relevant? (1-5)
3. Is communication frequency appropriate? (1-5)
4. Do you know who to ask when you have questions? (Y/N)
5. What communication format do you prefer? (Multiple choice)
6. What topics need more communication? (Open-ended)
7. What topics need less communication? (Open-ended)
8. Additional feedback? (Open-ended)
```

**2. Engagement Metrics**
- Email open rates
- Newsletter click-through rates
- Slack reactions and replies
- Wiki/documentation page views
- All-hands meeting attendance
- Question volume after announcements

**3. Direct Feedback Channels**
- Regular 1-on-1s with reports
- Team retrospectives
- Skip-level meetings
- Town hall Q&A sessions
- Dedicated feedback Slack channel
- Communication office hours

**4. Response Time Tracking**
- Time to acknowledge questions
- Time to provide full answers
- Clarity of initial responses
- Need for follow-up clarification

**5. Content Analysis**
- Questions asked after announcements
- Topics requiring most clarification
- Recurring confusion points
- Information gaps

**Acting on Feedback**:
- Share feedback summary with team
- Identify top 3 improvement areas
- Create action plan with owners
- Implement changes incrementally
- Measure impact of changes
- Close the loop on what changed
